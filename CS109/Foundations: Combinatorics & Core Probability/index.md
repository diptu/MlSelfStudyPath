# Study Plan: Stanford CS109 (Probability for Computer Scientists)

This repository contains a structured study plan to master the fundamentals of Probability and its applications in Machine Learning and Software Engineering. The curriculum is based on the Stanford CS109 2022 course.

## 📅 Weekly Schedule

### Week 1: Foundations & Combinatorics
**Goal:** Master the art of counting and the fundamental axioms of probability.
- **Topics:** Counting (Product & Sum rules), Permutations, Combinations, Probability Axioms, Conditional Probability, Bayes' Theorem, and Independence.
- **Lectures:** 1, 2, 3, 4, 5.
- **Key Outcome:** Ability to calculate the probability of complex events using tree diagrams and algebraic sets.

### Week 2: Discrete Random Variables
**Goal:** Model discrete phenomena and understand their properties.
- **Topics:** Probability Mass Functions (PMF), Cumulative Distribution Functions (CDF), Expectation, Variance, Bernoulli, Binomial, and Poisson Distributions.
- **Lectures:** 6, 7, 8.
- **Key Outcome:** Understanding how to model counts and binary outcomes in systems.

### Week 3: Continuous Random Variables & The CLT
**Goal:** Transition to continuous space and understand the most important theorem in statistics.
- **Topics:** Probability Density Functions (PDF), The Uniform and Normal (Gaussian) Distributions, Exponential Distribution, and the Central Limit Theorem (CLT).
- **Lectures:** 9, 10, 11.
- **Key Outcome:** Leveraging the Normal distribution to approximate discrete variables and perform error analysis.

### Week 4: Multivariate Distributions
**Goal:** Analyze systems with multiple interacting random variables.
- **Topics:** Joint PMFs/PDFs, Marginalization, Independence, Covariance, Correlation, and Conditional Expectation.
- **Lectures:** 12, 13, 14, 15.
- **Key Outcome:** Calculating dependencies between features in a dataset.

### Week 5: Parameter Estimation & Statistical Inference
**Goal:** Learn how to "learn" from data by estimating model parameters.
- **Topics:** The Bootstrap, Point Estimation, Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE), Bayesian Inference (MAP), and the Beta Distribution.
- **Lectures:** 16, 17, 18, 19.
- **Key Outcome:** Deriving parameters for a distribution that best fit a given dataset.

### Week 6: Machine Learning Applications & Ethics
**Goal:** Implement probabilistic algorithms for real-world tasks.
- **Topics:** Naive Bayes, Logistic Regression, Deep Learning (Probabilistic Perspective), and AI Fairness/Ethics.
- **Lectures:** 20, 21, 22, 23, 24.
- **Key Outcome:** Building a classifier and evaluating its societal impact and bias.

---

## 🛠 Prerequisites
- **Coding:** Proficiency in Python (NumPy/Matplotlib).
- **Math:** Multivariate Calculus (Partial derivatives and integration).
